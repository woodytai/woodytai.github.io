United Nations Security Council Resolution HKBUMUN 2025

Navigating the Political Transition and the De-escalation of Conflicted Parties in Syria

Sponsors: Russian Federation, United States of America

Signatories: People's Republic of China, Democratic Republic of Algeria, Republic of Panama

The United Nations Security Council,

Expressing grave concern at the situation in the Syrian Arab Republic and condemning the violence and use of force against civilians,

Acknowledging the Democratic Republic of Algeria's work on providing a platform for peace negotiation,

Considering that the widespread and systematic attacks that have taken place in the Syrian Arab Republic against the civilian population may amount to crimes against humanity,

Guided by the commitment to the establishment of a clear phased plan with deadlines towards achieving Syrian self-determination after a ceasefire has been achieved and terrorist threats are adequately removed.

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

01. Acting under Chapter VII of the Charter of the United Nations, and taking measures under Article 41,

a. Demands an immediate end to the violence and calls for steps to fulfil the legitimate demands of the population;

b. Urges the Syrian Interim government to

i. Act with the utmost restraint, respect human rights and international humanitarian law, and allow immediate access for international human rights monitors;

ii. Ensure the safe passage of humanitarian and medical supplies, and humanitarian agencies and workers, into the country; and;

iii. Cooperate in the evacuation of those foreign nationals wishing to leave the country;

02. ICC Referral

a. Decides to refer the situation in the Syrian Arab Republic to the Prosecutor of the International Criminal Court;

b. Decides that the Syrian authorities shall cooperate fully with and provide any necessary assistance to the Court and the Prosecutor under this resolution and,

i. Recognizing that States not party to the Rome Statute have no obligation under the Statute, urges all States and concerned regional and other international organizations to cooperate fully with the Court and the Prosecutor;

c. Invites the Prosecutor to address the Security Council within two months of the adoption of this resolution and every six months thereafter on actions taken under this resolution;

d. Recognises that the costs of this investigation will be born upon parties of the Rome Statue and states wishing to donate voluntarily under Appendix I

03. Withdrawal of Non-Domestic Military Forces

a. Urges the signing of an initial long-term ceasefire agreement between all major conflicting parties such as but not limited to: HTS, and YPG under UN mediation and guidance to establish a sustainable and permanent peace respected by all parties that can facilitate the ground for a mediation process.

b. Urges all terrorist organizations to begin the de-escalation and disarmament measures to reintegrate into society as decided by the Syrian interim government.

c. Decides to obtain permission from the interim Syrian government to engage in an approved military intervention as stipulated by article 42 of the UN charter if terrorist organizations refuse to disarm and continue their disruptive act

d. Urges UNSC permanent members to engage in air and naval military interventions as a last resort to stop terrorist forces once full approval has been obtained from the interim Syrian government working in conjunction with the interim Syrian military to obtain approval for any military action against terrorist forces in Syria in the interest of regional peace

04. Urges Member States to participate in an arms embargo

a. Decides that all Member States shall immediately take the necessary measures to prevent the direct or indirect supply, sale or transfer to the Syrian Arab Republic of arms and related materiel of all types, including weapons and ammunition, military vehicles and equipment, paramilitary equipment, and spare parts related to military activities or the like. Including the provision of armed mercenary personnel whether or not originating in their territories, and decides further that this measure shall not apply to:

i. Supplies of non-lethal military equipment intended solely for humanitarian or protective use.

ii. Materials Used By the United Nations Peacekeeping Force Reployed under Clause 3 of this Resolution

iii. Protective equipment for representatives of the media and humanitarian and development workers and associated personnel, for their personal use only; or

b. Calls upon all States, in particular States neighbouring the Syrian Arab Republic, to inspect, by their national authorities and legislation and consistent with international law, in particular the law of the sea and relevant international civil aviation agreements, all cargo to and from the Syrian Arab Republic.

c. Decides to authorize all Member States to, and that all Member States shall, upon discovery of items prohibited by 04 a, seize and dispose of (such as through destruction, rendering inoperable, storage or transferring to a State other than the originating or destination States for disposal)

i. Member states shall appear before the Security Council to report on the discovery of materials.

05. Commands the Organisation for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons to Investigate and if needed request UN Peacekeepers to aid in their investigation and if needed confiscate and destroy any chemical weapons.

06. Urges Member States to lift critical sanctions on essential goods against Syria, including but not limited to:

a. Food, medicine and energy;

b. Encourages the provision of humanitarian aid to Syria through the already established UN crisis response mechanism

LONG-TERM ACTIONS (6-12 months)

07. Initiate face-to-face negotiations between leaders of all conflicting parties to engage in a meeting in a neutral country to:

a. Ensure cooperation and eliminate the possibility of accidental violations of conflicting interests to establish a sustainable peace for the democratic process

b. Establish the framework for parliamentary democracy and the democratic process in Syria, with a UN advisor leveraging the insights gained by mediation, with all conflicting parties and the initial UN survey performed on day zero, referencing currently successful parliamentary democracies such as the US, Switzerland, Canada, Italy the Netherlands and more to lay the groundwork for the building of a secure and stable system.

08. Encourage all conflicting parties to commit to the long-term cessation of hostilities to stabilize the long-term stability of the ceasefire deal by:

a. Mandating that all conflicting parties engage in mutual activities to reduce the risk of hostilities being reignited by participating in the process of de-risking

i. Stipulating mutual escalation between all conflicting parties by pulling back armed forces from conflicting zones and disarmament of set parts to reduce the risk of armed conflict.

ii. Promoting confidence-building measures as part of the derisking process by establishing joint humanitarian aid relief missions in collaboration with existing UN efforts between the conflicted parties and counterterrorism operations under the observation and monitoring of the UN to build stability and trust, further reducing the risk of potential conflict.

iii. Having all parties affirm the single democratic framework as established

iv. Engage in the active mediation of major conflicting issues between the conflicting parties, including any disputes over sovereignty territory and human rights, to reach an amicable goal conclusion for all parties that pushes for continued de-escalation and integration into a single political system.

b. Having the transitional government of the HTS confirm its commitment to step down after the democratic process is successfully achieved in an open letter to all conflicting parties and the rest of the international community, ensuring that there will be no struggles during the transition of power from the current interim government to the permanent government

c. Establishing various official political parties based on the current conflicting parties to convert them into legitimate political organizations, running for election and resolving the dispute over sovereignty via a fair election as decided by the people

d. Begin the establishment of an official government structure through collaboration between all conflicting parties, the HTS and the UN with a separate legislative executive and judicial branch, encouraging a fair democratic process and separation of powers;

(12+ MONTHS)

09. Mandating that the UNSC reconvene in 12 months to assess the effectiveness of the above-stated measures and draft a new plan extending the current resolution on how to achieve Syrian self-determination based on the success of the measures achieved above.
