Research Question/Engineering Objectives

Hydrogen is proposed as a zero-carbon fuel alternative, but most commercially-produced hydrogen comes from fossil fuels, which harms the environment.


Engineering Problem: We aim to prepare an effective, low-cost, dual-functional bimetallic-sulphide catalyst to replace traditional expensive catalysts in Green-hydrogen production.


Objective: Synthesise an electrocatalyst to increase the efficiency of Green-hydrogen production


Engineering Criteria(Measure success):

  • ☐ Analyze the onset potential of our electrocatalyst
  • ☐ Study the durability of proposed CuNiS electrocatalyst
  • ☐ Grow the catalyst in-situ

Methodology/Project Design

  • Step 1: Prepare Cu(OH)2 Nanorod Arrays on Copper Foam by Alkaline Oxidation [(NH4)2S2O8 / OH-]
  • Step 2: Transform Cu(OH)2 to CuxNi(1-x)S Nanorod Arrays on Copper Foam by Hydrothermal Heating
  • Step 3: Investigate the Material Characterizations by SEM and SEM-EDX analysis to understand the surface chemical composition
  • Step 4: Analyze results from electrochemical studies via Linear Sweep Voltomotry(LSV) by this equation ERHE = EAg/AgCl + 5.91•10-2 x pH + E⊖Ag/AgCl

Interpretation & Conclusions

  • CuxNi(1-x)S@Cu nanoarrays electrode exhibits promising HER activity
  • The transformation of 1D Cu(OH)2@Cu to 1D CuxNi(1-x)S@Cu without serious structural deterioration can only be achieved in optimum conditions (120°C, 10mM Ni2+, 5nM THiourea).
  • NiS35 and NiS40 catalysts display the best performance, with the lowest potential required to achieve considerable current density (only 1.417 V for UOR, and -0.271 for HER at 100 mA cm-2)
